Subhas Chandra Bose

The man who taught India to Fight.

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose.

Here's a time line of Subhas Chandra Bose's life:

  • 1897 - Born in Cuttack,Orissa,India.
  • 1902 - He was admitted to the Protestant European School.
  • 1918 - He later joined the Scottish Church College at the University of Calcutta and passed his B.A. in philosophy
  • 1919 - After studies,he came fourth in the ICS examination and was selected.
  • 1921 - He did not want to work under an alien government which would mean serving the British and resigned from Indian Civil Services.
  • 1927 - Bose became general secretary of the Congress party and worked with Jawaharlal Nehru for independence.
  • 1939 - Bose appeared at the Congress meeting on a stretcher. He was elected president again over Gandhi's preferred candidate Pattabhi Sitaramayya.[48] U. Muthuramalingam Thevar strongly supported Bose in the intra-Congress dispute.
  • 1941 - Bose escaped to Germany via Afganistan,after being arrested by British Government.
  • 1942 - He created Indian National Army(INA) to fight the British Government.
  • 1943 - Bose went to Japan to seek help for INA from Japanese government.
  • 1944 - Spoken as a part of a motivational speech for the Indian National Army at a rally of Indians in Burma, Bose's most famous quote was "Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom!".
  • 1945 - Dies in a plane crash.

"Subhas Chandra Bose was an Indian nationalist whose defiant patriotism made him a hero in India.He was a great soul."
